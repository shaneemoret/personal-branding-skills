# Speaker Bio Example

## User Input

`I need a speaker bio portrait for a founder speaking at business events. Use the reference photo. I want it to feel authoritative, polished, and credible without looking stiff.`

## Recommended Visual Direction

Authoritative with controlled warmth, because a speaker portrait needs stronger presence than a LinkedIn thumbnail while still feeling human and approachable.

## Output Highlights

### Use Case

- Speaker / press
- Primary ratio: 16:9

### Prompt Strategy

- Lock identity first from the reference photo
- Use higher-contrast lighting than the default
- Keep posture and composition broader than LinkedIn
- Preserve realism over polish

### Example Base Prompt

Create a photorealistic professional speaker bio portrait of the subject from the reference image. Preserve the subject's exact likeness, including facial structure, skin tone, age, hairline, and signature features. The subject should appear composed and authoritative with direct eye contact and a calm, confident expression that does not feel stern. Dress the subject in a dark suit jacket and crisp white shirt. Frame as a 3/4 body portrait with the subject offset one-third from the frame edge for speaker and press usage. Use key light high camera left, a clear shadow triangle on the cheek, minimal fill, subtle rim light on the shoulder, and deep catchlights so the eyes stay alive. Use a dark neutral gradient or softly blurred office background that does not compete with the subject. Capture with 85mm compression, shallow depth of field, natural skin texture, 4K output. Avoid plastic skin, uncanny eyes, distorted lapels, or AI corporate composite styling.

## Likely Failure Mode

The portrait may become too stern or too editorial.

## Fix Instruction

`Keep the expression calm and authoritative, but soften the mouth and eyes slightly so the subject still feels approachable and real.`
