# Content Signal Evaluator Examples

Use these to calibrate scoring and upgrade quality, not to force future evaluations into the same categories.

## Example 1: Weak Idea -> Upgraded Claim

**Input**
- Idea: AI is changing marketing
- Goal: Attract better-fit B2B consulting leads
- ICP: B2B consultants
- Preferred belief target: Belief Refinement

**Output**

### Idea Summary
- Working title: AI is changing marketing
- Core claim: AI is changing marketing
- Rating: NOT READY

### Scorecard
- Claim Strength: FAIL
- Upstream Test: FAIL
- Falsifiability Test: FAIL
- Mechanism Test: FAIL
- Provenance Test: FAIL
- Evidence Density Test: FAIL
- Scar Tissue Test: FAIL
- ICP Resonance Test: WEAK
- Belief-State Fit Test: WEAK
- Strategic Utility Test: WEAK
- Format Leverage Test: WEAK
- Information Gain Test: FAIL
- Trust Visibility Test: FAIL
- Intent Satisfaction Potential Test: FAIL
- Opinion Ladder: 1

### Diagnosis
- Primary failure mode: Topic Bucket
- Buyer-fit quality: weak-fit
- Best format: none yet
- Best belief target: Belief Refinement

### Upgrade
- Upgraded claim: AI is not making expert consultants more visible by default. It is making undocumented expertise invisible, because recommendation systems can only surface thinking that has been externalized into publishable proof.
- What would make this STRONG: Add a repeated observed pattern, proof from client or market behavior, and a clearer mechanism for how selection actually happens.
