# ICP Visual Concept Generator Examples

Use these examples to calibrate output quality, not to constrain every future response to the same categories or metaphors.

## Fast Mode Example

**Input**
- Content Idea: How AI search decides which consultant gets recommended
- ICP: B2B consultants
- Platform: LinkedIn
- Tone: not provided
- Brand Positioning: Luxury / editorial
- Brand Colors: not provided
- Website URL: not provided

**Output**

1. **Sharpened Idea**
AI search does not reward credentials alone. It rewards visible, documented thinking, which makes invisible experts functionally absent.

2. **Visual Strategy**
TENSION / CONFLICT - the claim is about selection pressure and visibility asymmetry, so the image should dramatize who gets surfaced and who disappears.

3. **Platform Spec**
LinkedIn 4:5, top-heavy composition, default to no text unless the concept is unclear without it.

4. **Production Prompt**
```text
Cinematic editorial photograph. A single consultant seated upright at a long dark oak boardroom table under a tight overhead spotlight while five equally qualified peers recede into shadow. Private-equity-style boardroom with printed proposals, bound transcripts, annotated workshop decks, and closed notebooks instead of laptops. Warm overhead tungsten spotlight, heavy surrounding falloff, shallow depth of field, centered 4:5 crop with the lit subject in the upper half of frame. Near-black primary, warm walnut secondary, muted brass accent. Tense, selective, high-status mood. 50mm editorial photography, natural texture, restrained contrast, no text overlay.
```

5. **Color source**
Color source: Inferred from ICP/Brand

## Full Strategy Example

**Input**
- Content Idea: Why most founders hit $2M and stop growing
- ICP: Founders who have scaled past $1M and feel stuck
- Platform: YouTube
- Tone: Contrarian
- Brand Positioning: Operator / direct
- Brand Fonts: not provided
- Website URL: not provided

**Output**

1. **Sharpened Idea**
Most founders do not hit a systems ceiling at $2M. They hit a decision ceiling and mistake it for an operations problem.

2. **ICP Psychology Map**
- Fear: The real bottleneck in the company is them, not the market
- Desire: A structural explanation that preserves their identity as a capable operator
- Identity Signal: Serious founder, still in control, not a victim of chaos

3. **Visual Strategy**
TENSION / CONFLICT

4. **Image Function**
Selection Pressure

5. **Platform Spec + Typography**
YouTube 16:9. Use a face, one strong emotional read, and a short text overlay. Assuming neutral sans based on operator/direct positioning.

6. **Scene Concept A**
- Environment: Glass-walled founder office overlooking an active team floor
- Subject: Founder standing just inside the office, hand on the door frame
- State or action: Watching the company operate without fully entering it
- Emotional register: Controlled discomfort
- Composition: Subject left third, negative space on right for text, team softened in the background
- Brand layer: Standing desk, printed scorecard, half-erased quarterly priorities, understated dark wardrobe

7. **Scene Concept B**
- Environment: Clean operations room with KPI screens and a whiteboard
- Subject: Founder seated alone at the far end of a conference table
- State or action: One chair occupied, all other chairs empty, growth model open but untouched
- Emotional register: Isolation under control
- Composition: Wider frame, centered vanishing lines, tighter crop on face and table edge
- Brand layer: Matte black notebook, printed P&L, capped marker, no startup cliches

8. **Recommended Concept**
Scene A. It resolves faster at thumbnail size because the founder, the glass barrier, and the active team create one immediate tension instead of requiring the viewer to decode a more static tableau. It also gives cleaner space for short YouTube text without compromising the scene.

9. **Why It Works**
This ICP resists messaging that makes them look incompetent. The concept preserves competence while surfacing a harder tension: they built the system but are now separated from its next growth stage by their own decision role. That creates click tension without humiliating the viewer.

10. **Production Prompt**
```text
Cinematic business photograph. A founder in their late 30s stands inside a glass-walled office, one hand resting on the partially open door frame while their team works in soft focus beyond the glass. Modern operator-focused office with a standing desk, printed weekly scorecard, half-erased quarterly priorities on a whiteboard, and a closed matte black laptop. Diffused daylight from floor-to-ceiling windows with a subtle interior edge light, medium depth of field, 16:9 composition with subject on the left third and clean negative space on the right for text. Charcoal primary, cool gray secondary, sharp white accent. Controlled, high-stakes, self-confronting mood. 35mm editorial photography. Typography: neutral sans, bold, all caps, right side placement, 3-5 words max.
```

Color source: Inferred from ICP/Brand

11. **Anti-Patterns**
- For this ICP: avoid hustle-chaos desk imagery because it signals early-stage disorder, not a capable founder at a growth plateau.
- For this ICP: avoid celebratory team shots because they erase the internal bottleneck tension the claim depends on.
- For this category: avoid abstract chart overlays because they flatten a personal operating constraint into generic business content.

12. **Reuse Variants**
- LinkedIn: Remove text, tighten crop around founder and glass barrier, keep team blur in the upper background.
- Blog: Preserve the full 16:9 scene, move subject slightly left, reserve right half for headline overlay.
- Newsletter: Compress to 3:1, keep founder face and glass edge, drop most of the team floor while preserving motion blur.
